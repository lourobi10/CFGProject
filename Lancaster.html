Lancaster


Lancaster is the county town of Lancashire, situated in the North West of England on the River Lune.

It has a population of 52,234.

Lancaster has a long history, with a Roman Fort established where the medieval castle still stands today.

In 2004, Lancaster was granted Fairtrade City status.

Places to Visit

Lancaster Castle

Built on the site of the original Roman fort in the 11th Century, Lancaster Castle offers 45 minute tours taking visitors back through the castle's history of royal visits, witchcraft and crime & punishment.

Visit Lancaster Castle
Lancaster Museum

Located in the City Centre, this free museum houses the art, archaeology and military collections that tell the story of Lancaster's past.

Visit Lancaster Museum
Williamsons Park

Overlooking the City of Lancaster, Williamsons Park offers 54 acres of woodland trails, a Butterfly House, Small Animal Zoo and Cafe. The site hosts various outdoor events throughout the year.

Visit Williamsons Park
